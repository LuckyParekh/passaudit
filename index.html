    Password Best Practices

    📝

    Use Passphrases

    String 4–5 random words together. "correct-horse-battery-staple" is both memorable and strong.

    🔁

    Never Reuse Passwords

    One breach can expose every account you have. Each service deserves its own unique password.

    🙅

    Avoid Personal Info

    Names, birthdays, and pet names are guessed first. Keep your passwords free of any personal identifiers.

    🗄️

    Use a Password Manager

    Tools like Bitwarden, 1Password, or KeePass generate and store strong unique passwords for you automatically.

    🔐

    Enable 2FA Everywhere

    Two-factor authentication is your last line of defense even if a password is compromised.

    🔄

    Rotate Sensitive Passwords

    Change passwords for critical accounts (email, banking) every 6–12 months, especially after any data breach.
